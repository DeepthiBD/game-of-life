# XP-005 — Parent Trust Framework

**Version**: 1.0  
**Status**: Active — Binding on all modules, current and future  
**Authority**: This document overrides growth-hacking, engagement, retention, and monetization decisions.

---

## Core Belief

> **LifePilot exists to support children.**

- LifePilot does **not** exist to maximize screen time
- LifePilot does **not** exist to manipulate behaviour
- LifePilot does **not** replace parents
- LifePilot supports conversations between children and parents

---

## The Ten Trust Principles

### Principle 1 — Privacy First
Child reflections, journals, and Future Me letters remain **private by default**.  
No hidden monitoring. No surveillance. No behavioural profiling. No advertising networks.

### Principle 2 — Offline First
LifePilot functions without internet access. Offline-first architecture is a **trust feature** — parents feel confident the app is not continuously collecting information.

### Principle 3 — No Social Comparison
Never compare children. No public rankings, leaderboards, popularity systems, follower counts, or social pressure loops.

### Principle 4 — No Manipulative Retention
No addiction loops. No infinite streak pressure, fear of losing progress, artificial scarcity, excessive notifications, dark patterns, or psychological pressure tactics.

### Principle 5 — Growth Over Performance
Celebrate effort, reflection, learning, exploration, resilience, character, kindness, and curiosity.  
Avoid performance obsession, constant scoring, and excessive metrics.

### Principle 6 — Future-Oriented Optimism
Every module helps children believe: their future can improve, mistakes are learning opportunities, growth is possible, and the future is not fixed.

### Principle 7 — Parents Are Co-Pilots
Parents are not auditors. Parents are not supervisors. Parents are **co-pilots**.  
LifePilot encourages healthy family conversations: career, money, values, future, and challenges.

### Principle 8 — Child Autonomy
Children have ownership. Children make choices. Children set goals. Children reflect voluntarily.  
Children should not feel constantly evaluated.

### Principle 9 — Safe AI Principles
AI must guide, encourage, educate, clarify, and reflect.  
AI must **never** diagnose, label children, predict life outcomes, replace human relationships, or create dependency.

### Principle 10 — Parent Transparency
Parents understand: why information is collected, where it is stored, how it is used, how it can be deleted, and what remains private.

## Trust Tests

### Parent Perception Test
1. Would a parent feel comfortable sitting beside their child using this?
2. Would a parent recommend this to another parent?
3. Would a parent believe this builds character?
4. Would a parent believe this encourages positive life choices?

If any answer is NO → Redesign.

### School Trust Test
1. Would a school believe this supports life skills?
2. Would a school believe this aligns with future readiness?
3. Would a school believe this promotes student wellbeing?

If not → Redesign.

### Child Trust Test
1. Would a child feel safe?
2. Would a child feel respected?
3. Would a child feel encouraged?
4. Would a child feel ownership?
5. Would a child feel hopeful?

If not → Redesign.

- Premium content
- Premium learning paths
- Family packs

- Premium experiences

### ❌ Not Allowed
- Selling child data
- Advertising networks
- Behavioural advertising
- Manipulative upsells
- Pay-to-progress systems

## Screen Review Checklist

Before any screen is approved:
1. Builds trust
2. Protects privacy
3. Encourages growth
4. Avoids shame
5. Avoids comparison
6. Avoids manipulation
7. Encourages family conversations
8. Child remains the hero
9. Parent remains a co-pilot
10. Future remains hopeful
