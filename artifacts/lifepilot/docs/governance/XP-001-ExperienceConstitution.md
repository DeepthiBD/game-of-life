# XP-001 — LifePilot Experience & Visual Design Constitution

**Version**: 1.0  
**Status**: Active — Binding on all UI decisions  
**Authority**: This document overrides all generic UI decisions.  
**Scope**: All modules, screens, components, and interactions

---

## What LifePilot IS

**LifePilot is a Life Navigation Adventure Platform.**

The child is the hero. The child is the Pilot. The app is their aircraft. Life is the journey. The future is the horizon.

---

## What LifePilot is NOT

| ❌ Not this | ✅ This instead |
|------------|----------------|
| School ERP | Adventure platform |
| Productivity app | Life navigation journey |
| LMS | Discovery experience |
| Assessment platform | Growth exploration |
| Monitoring system | Empowerment tool |

---

## Core Experience Philosophy

The app should feel like:
- **Adventure** — every session is an expedition
- **Discovery** — new things to find around every corner
- **Growth** — the child visibly becoming more
- **Hope** — the future is bright and reachable
- **Exploration** — wide open skies, not narrow corridors
- **Possibility** — anything is achievable

The app must NOT feel like:
- Homework
- Assessment
- Monitoring
- Corporate productivity
- School administration

---

## The Golden Rule

> **Before generating any screen, ask: "Would a child willingly open this after school?"**
>
> If the answer is no — redesign.

---

## Emotional Goals

Every screen must create at least one of:

| Emotion | How |
|---------|-----|
| Curiosity | Tease what's around the corner |
| Wonder | Beautiful, surprising visuals |
| Hope | Future-oriented language and imagery |
| Confidence | Celebrate every step, not just milestones |
| Exploration | Open pathways, not closed forms |
| Achievement | Visible progress and earned rewards |
| Reflection | Gentle, non-judgmental prompts |
| Optimism | Language of possibility |
| Future Thinking | Connect today's action to tomorrow's self |

## Module Experience Standards

### Cockpit (Home)
- Must feel alive, welcoming, and optimistic
- Displays:
  - "Welcome, Captain [Name]"
  - Journey Progress
  - Today's Mission
  - Message From Future You
  - Continue Adventure
  - Explore New Paths
  - Upcoming Milestones
- Visual: Large cards, not KPI tiles
- Tone: Warm, exciting, like the first day of an adventure

### Future Me
- Flagship feature — must feel **magical**
- Visual ideas: sunrise horizon, letter floating toward viewer, warm encouraging imagery
- Language: "Message From Future You", "Future Horizon", "Letter Waiting", "Journey Through Time"
- Never: "Future Letter #123" or clinical labels

### Flight Log (Reflection)
- Feels like talking to a trusted friend — not a teacher
- Use: conversation prompts, quick reflections, mood check-ins, story cards
- Example prompts:
  - "What surprised you today?"
  - "What made you smile today?"
  - "What would Future You thank you for?"
  - "What challenge helped you grow?"
- Avoid: essay prompts, long forms, school-assignment feel

### Career Explorer
- Careers are **worlds to explore**, not database entries
- Display as Career Worlds:
  - ⚖️ Law World
  - 🚀 Space World
  - 🏥 Health World
  - 💻 Technology World
  - 🎨 Design World
  - 📈 Entrepreneurship World
- Career roadmaps = adventure paths
- Never: large tables, academic catalogues, career databases

### Money Quest
- Must never feel like a finance course
- Feels like: choices, challenges, stories, adventures, decision scenarios
- Use: coins, treasures, rewards, future choices, consequence reveals
- Never: balance sheets, interest rate tables, financial jargon

### Life Choices
- Scenario-based interactions
- Example: "You have ₹100. Choose: 🍕 Enjoy now / 📚 Learn something / 💰 Save it / ❤️ Help someone"
- Reveal outcomes visually with consequence storytelling
- Not like quizzes — more like choose-your-own-adventure

## Gamification Rules

### ✅ Allowed
- Milestones
- Explorer Badges
- Journey Progress
- Growth Moments
- Adventure Paths
- Discovery Rewards

### ❌ Not Allowed
- Leaderboards (no competition between children)
- Ranking Students
- Competitive Pressure
- Infinite Streak Pressure (streaks are gentle, never punishing)
- Dark Patterns
- Manipulative Retention mechanics

---

## Visual Design Principles

### Use
- Large visual cards
- Friendly illustrations
- Storytelling elements
- Journey metaphors: maps, paths, compasses, horizons, clouds, stars
- Badges and exploration elements
- Gradients (sparingly)
- Warm, inviting colour

### Avoid
- Tables
- Spreadsheets
- Corporate dashboards
- Complex charts
- Dense text blocks
- Administrative layouts
- School report card appearance
- Dark enterprise themes

---

## Visual Style Reference

**Feel**: Pixar · Disney Adventure · Modern Educational · Calm Exploration · Friendly Storytelling

**Not**: Corporate SaaS · Banking App · ERP · School Management System

## Colour Palette

| Role | Colour | Feel |
|------|--------|------|
| Primary | Sky Blue | Open skies, possibility, calm |
| Secondary | Sunrise Orange | Energy, warmth, new beginnings |
| Accent | Adventure Yellow | Excitement, discovery, joy |
| Success | Forest Green | Growth, achievement, nature |
| Background | Warm White | Clean, safe, inviting |

- Use gradients sparingly (Sky Blue → Sunrise Orange for hero moments)
- Avoid dark enterprise themes
- Light mode is the default; dark mode is optional

---

## Illustration Strategy

Every major screen must contain one of:
- An illustration
- A journey element (path, horizon, map)
- A character element (Pilot avatar, companion)
- A visual storytelling element

Avoid icon-only interfaces — illustrations create emotional connection.

---

## Mobile First

Target: 10–16 year old users on mobile

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|---------|
| Touch targets | Large (minimum 44px, prefer 56px) |
| Cards | Large, full-width on mobile |
| Buttons | Prominent, easy to tap |
| Typing | Minimise — prefer tap/swipe interactions |
| Interaction length | Short — max 3 taps to complete any core action |
| Reading level | Age-appropriate — simple, encouraging language |

---

## Accessibility

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| Reading level | Child-friendly (Grade 5–8 reading level target) |
| Typography | Readable, well-spaced, generous line height |
| Contrast | High contrast — minimum WCAG 4.5:1 |
| Touch targets | Large (56px preferred for primary actions) |
| Dyslexia-friendly fonts | Planned — Wave 2 |
| Motion | Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` |

## Perception Tests

### Child Perception Test ✅
The child must think:
- "This is my adventure."
- "This is fun."
- "I want to come back."

### Parent Perception Test ✅
The parent must think:
- "This is positive."
- "This builds character."
- "This is safe."
- "This encourages reflection."

The parent must NOT think:
- "This feels addictive."

### School Perception Test ✅
The school must think:
- "This supports life skills."
- "This supports NEP goals."
- "This builds future readiness."
